### 🏠 **Survival Basics**

**Learn the House Layout**: Familiarize yourself with the house's layout before the night begins. Remember the route between the main living areas and the front door. This will help you investigate changes without getting lost.

**Inspect the Starting Environment**: Take note of what appears normal at the start of the night. This will serve as a reference point for recognizing changes later in the game.

**Approach Visitors Carefully**: Do not immediately let visitors inside. Move to the door, observe the visitor, and wait for the available interaction or dialogue before deciding.

**Look for Anomalies**: Compare the visitor's appearance, behavior, and statements with what you have already seen. Unusual details or inconsistent information can indicate that the encounter is dangerous.

**Make the Door Decision**: Choose whether to trust or reject the visitor only after completing your inspection. Allowing the wrong person inside or rejecting an important visitor can change the outcome of the night.

**Repeat the Survival Loop**: After each encounter, check the house again and prepare for the next visitor. Continue observing, comparing, and deciding until the current sequence reaches its ending.

### 🧐 **Visitor Inspection**

**Normal-Looking Visitor**: A visitor may look ordinary and speak calmly without showing an immediate visual anomaly. However, their explanation may still be vague, inconsistent, or unrelated to the reason they came to the house.

**Familiar-Looking Visitor**: Some visitors may appear recognizable or attempt to gain trust through familiarity. However, unnatural expressions, strange wording, unusual timing, or behavior that does not match the person they resemble should be treated as red flags.

**Visitor With Abnormal Features**: A dangerous visitor can display distorted facial details, unnatural proportions, or other visible abnormalities. Eyes, mouth, posture, movement, or body shape that appear noticeably wrong should prompt caution.

**Aggressive Visitor**: An unsafe visitor may pressure the player, become threatening, or demand an immediate response. Hostile language, repeated demands, sudden changes in tone, or attempts to prevent careful inspection are signs of a dangerous encounter.

**Visitor With an Inconsistent Story**: The visitor gives a reason for arriving, but parts of the explanation conflict with their behavior or the situation. Contradictory dialogue, missing details, or statements that change during the conversation should be treated as a danger signal.

**Silent or Unresponsive Visitor**: A person may remain unusually quiet or fail to respond normally during the door interaction. Long silence, fixed staring, limited dialogue, or no believable explanation for being present should prompt caution.

**Visitor Appearing at an Unusual Time**: A visitor can arrive when their presence does not fit the current point in the night. The timing feels unexplained, the visitor acts as though the situation is normal, or their reason for arriving is weak.

**Visitor Followed by a House Change**: The danger may become clear only after the conversation ends and the player returns inside. New sounds, altered rooms, unusual figures, changed lighting, or objects that were not present before should prompt immediate inspection.

### 🔍 **Anomaly Detection**

**Unexpected Figure**: A person or human-like shape appears in a place that was previously empty. The figure remains motionless, watches the player, appears suddenly, or has unnatural features.

**Changed Furniture or Objects**: Furniture, decorations, or household items appear moved, replaced, missing, or visually altered. An object no longer matches its original position, shape, number, or appearance.

**Abnormal Face or Body**: A character displays facial or physical details that do not look human or consistent. Distorted eyes, mouth, limbs, posture, proportions, or expressions should prompt caution.

**Lighting Change**: The brightness, color, or behavior of the lights changes without a normal cause. Sudden darkness, flickering, uneven lighting, or a room looking different from the previous inspection should prompt caution.

**Window Anomaly**: A figure, movement, or visual detail appears outside or directly against a window. A face, silhouette, unexplained motion, or object appears where nothing was visible before.

**Door Anomaly**: A door behaves differently, reveals something unusual, or becomes connected to a suspicious visitor event. Unexpected opening, repeated knocking, unusual sounds, or a dangerous figure visible nearby should prompt caution.

**Unexplained Sound**: Footsteps, knocking, movement, or another sound occurs without a visible source. The sound repeats, moves between rooms, or begins immediately after a visitor encounter should prompt caution.

**Environment No Longer Matches**: Multiple details in a room change together, making the space feel noticeably different from its original state. Altered objects, lighting, figures, or room details should prompt caution.

### 🛡️ **Survival Tips**

- **Never Decide Immediately**: Wait until you have inspected the visitor and read the full conversation. A rushed door choice can end the run before important warning signs become clear.
- **Check the Visitor's Appearance**: Look closely for unusual facial features, body details, movement, or other visual inconsistencies before allowing anyone into the house.
- **Read Every Dialogue Line**: Visitor dialogue can provide clues about identity, intentions, or contradictions. Skipping text makes suspicious encounters harder to judge.
- **Compare Repeated Encounters**: A familiar visitor should not automatically be trusted. Compare their current appearance and behavior with earlier encounters because small differences may matter.
- **Recheck the House**: Environmental changes can appear between visitor encounters. Inspect nearby rooms and visible objects instead of remaining at the door for the entire night.
- **Use Sound as a Warning**: Pay attention to knocks, movement, and other audio cues. Unexpected sounds can signal a new visitor or a change somewhere inside the house.
- **Remember Previous Clues**: Keep track of what visitors said and how the house looked earlier. Later choices may depend on recognizing a contradiction or repeated anomaly.
- **Stay Near a Clear Route**: Learn the shortest route between the main rooms and the entrance. Moving confidently through the house makes repeated inspections faster and safer.
