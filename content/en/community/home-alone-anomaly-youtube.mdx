3. **Inspect Every Visitor Carefully**: When someone appears at the door, observe their appearance and read all dialogue before making a decision. Look for unnatural behavior, inconsistent statements, or details that do not match the situation.

5. **Avoid Dangerous Interactions**: Do not approach suspicious figures or interact with clearly abnormal events unless the game requires it. Create distance and use the safest available response when an encounter becomes threatening.
6. **Continue the Observation Cycle**: Repeat the door check, dialogue review, and house inspection process as the night progresses. Later encounters demand closer attention because the warning signs can become less obvious.
7. **Handle the Final Encounters**: Near the end of the night, use everything learned from earlier visitors and anomalies. Reject clearly unsafe choices and avoid taking unnecessary risks before the final sequence begins.
8. **Reach the Final Scene**: Survive the remaining sequence without inviting danger inside or approaching active threats. The final scene begins after the required encounters and inspection stages have been completed.

## 🌟 Visitors Guide: How to Judge Every Door Visitor
Visitor encounters are one of the main survival tests in Home Alone Anomaly. Here's how to judge every door visitor:

- **Normal-Looking Visitor**: A visitor may look ordinary and speak calmly without showing an immediate visual anomaly. Their explanation may still be vague, inconsistent, or unrelated to the reason they came to the house. Read every dialogue line and only continue when both their appearance and story remain consistent.
- **Familiar-Looking Visitor**: Some visitors may appear recognizable or attempt to gain trust through familiarity. Unnatural expressions, strange wording, unusual timing, or behavior that does not match the person they resemble should be treated as red flags. Judge the visitor by current evidence rather than recognition and refuse entry when details do not match.
- **Visitor With Abnormal Features**: A dangerous visitor can display distorted facial details, unnatural proportions, or other visible abnormalities. Eyes, mouth, posture, movement, or body shape that appear noticeably wrong should be a reason to not allow the visitor inside and avoid prolonged interaction after identifying the anomaly.
- **Aggressive Visitor**: An unsafe visitor may pressure the player, become threatening, or demand an immediate response. Hostile language, repeated demands, sudden changes in tone, or attempts to prevent careful inspection should lead to not responding to pressure by opening the door; choose the option that keeps the house secured.
- **Visitor With an Inconsistent Story**: The visitor gives a reason for arriving, but parts of the explanation conflict with their behavior or the situation. Contradictory dialogue, missing details, or statements that change during the conversation should be treated as a danger signal and deny access.
- **Silent or Unresponsive Visitor**: A person may remain unusually quiet or fail to respond normally during the door interaction. Long silence, fixed staring, limited dialogue, or no believable explanation for being present should lead to keeping the door closed and ending the encounter without approaching further.
- **Visitor Appearing at an Unusual Time**: A visitor can arrive when their presence does not fit the current point in the night. The timing feels unexplained, the visitor acts as though the situation is normal, or their reason for arriving is weak. Use the arrival time as an additional clue and reject the visitor when it conflicts with their story.
- **Visitor Followed by a House Change**: The danger may become clear only after the conversation ends and the player returns inside. New sounds, altered rooms, unusual figures, changed lighting, or objects that were not present before should lead to inspecting the house immediately after every encounter and responding to any new anomaly before continuing.

## 🌟 Anomalies Guide: Recognize Dangerous Changes Inside the House
Anomalies can appear through visual changes, suspicious characters, unusual sounds, or events that break the normal pattern of the house. Here's how to recognize dangerous changes inside the house:

- **Unexpected Figure**: A person or human-like shape appears in a place that was previously empty. The figure remains motionless, watches the player, appears suddenly, or has unnatural features. Do not approach it unnecessarily and follow the safest available route away from the area.
- **Changed Furniture or Objects**: Furniture, decorations, or household items appear moved, replaced, missing, or visually altered. An object no longer matches its original position, shape, number, or appearance. Treat the change as an active anomaly and avoid interacting with it unless progression requires it.
- **Abnormal Face or Body**: A character displays facial or physical details that do not look human or consistent. Distorted eyes, mouth, limbs, posture, proportions, or expressions should lead to rejecting the visitor or moving away from the character immediately.
- **Lighting Change**: The brightness, color, or behavior of the lights changes without a normal cause. Sudden darkness, flickering, uneven lighting, or a room looking different from the previous inspection should lead to inspecting the area from a safe distance and checking nearby rooms for additional changes.
- **Window Anomaly**: A figure, movement, or visual detail appears outside or directly against a window. A face, silhouette, unexplained motion, or object appears where nothing was visible before. Do not move directly toward the window and remain alert for a related encounter inside.
- **Door Anomaly**: A door behaves differently, reveals something unusual, or becomes connected to a suspicious visitor event. Unexpected opening, repeated knocking, unusual sounds, or a dangerous figure visible nearby should lead to keeping the entrance secured and inspecting before choosing any interaction.
- **Unexplained Sound**: Footsteps, knocking, movement, or another sound occurs without a visible source. The sound repeats, moves between rooms, or begins immediately after a visitor encounter. Check nearby areas carefully without rushing toward the source.
- **Environment No Longer Matches**: Multiple details in a room change together, making the space feel noticeably different from its original state. Altered objects, lighting, figures, or room details appear during the same inspection. Leave the area, avoid suspicious interactions, and continue only after identifying a safe route.

## 🌟 Endings Guide: How to Reach the Final Ending
Reaching the ending requires consistent decisions throughout the night rather than one final choice. Here's how to reach the final ending:

- **Complete the Night Sequence**: Keep the house secure, handle visitor encounters safely, and survive each anomaly event. Continue following the inspection cycle until the final scripted scene begins.
- **Avoid the Wrong Visitor**: Allowing a suspicious or abnormal visitor to gain access can trigger a dangerous encounter and end the run before the final scene. Check appearance, dialogue, and behavior before making the decision.
- **Familiarity Is Not Enough**: Familiarity is not enough to confirm that a visitor is safe. Unnatural features, inconsistent dialogue, or strange behavior should take priority over the visitor's appearance.
- **Early Failure Causes**: Common causes include opening the door for an unsafe visitor, approaching a threatening figure, ignoring an obvious environmental change, or selecting a risky interaction during an encounter.
- **Recheck the House**: New anomalies can appear after visitor conversations and other scripted events. Rechecking the same rooms allows the player to notice changes before entering a dangerous area.
- **Safest Visitor Strategy**: Keep the door closed until the visitor's appearance, dialogue, and reason for arriving all make sense. Refuse entry whenever one of those details appears abnormal or contradictory.
- **Survive the Final Sequence**: Avoid unnecessary exploration, maintain distance from suspicious figures, and continue choosing the safest available responses. Do not assume the danger has ended until the final scene has fully started.
- **Failed Run**: After a failed run, repeat the night while focusing on the encounter that caused the failure. Memorize the normal room layout, slow down during visitor dialogue, and inspect the house after every major event.

## 🌟 Survival Tips: How to Survive the Night
Survival depends on patience and observation rather than fast reactions. The safest approach is to inspect every visitor, compare new events with earlier scenes, and avoid treating repeated encounters as automatically identical. Here are some survival tips:

- **Never Decide Immediately**: Wait until you have inspected the visitor and read the full conversation. A rushed door choice can end the run before important warning signs become clear.
- **Check the Visitor's Appearance**: Look closely for unusual facial features, body details, movement, or other visual inconsistencies before allowing anyone into the house.
- **Read Every Dialogue Line**: Visitor dialogue can provide clues about identity, intentions, or contradictions. Skipping text makes suspicious encounters harder to judge.
- **Compare Repeated Encounters**: A familiar visitor should not automatically be trusted. Compare their current appearance and behavior with earlier encounters because small differences may matter.
- **Recheck the House**: Environmental changes can appear between visitor encounters. Inspect nearby rooms and visible objects instead of remaining at the door for the entire night.
- **Use Sound as a Warning**: Pay attention to knocks, movement, and other audio cues. Unexpected sounds can signal a new visitor or a change somewhere inside the house.
- **Remember Previous Clues**: Keep track of what visitors said and how the house looked earlier. Later choices may depend on recognizing a contradiction or repeated anomaly.
- **Stay Near a Clear Route**: Learn the shortest route between the main rooms and the entrance. Moving confidently through the house makes repeated inspections faster and safer.
