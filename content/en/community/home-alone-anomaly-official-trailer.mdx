# Home Alone Anomaly: Walkthrough, Visitors & Endings Guide

## Quick Guide
- **Primary keyword**: Home Alone Anomaly
- **Key mechanic**: Player must inspect visitors, identify anomalies, and survive the night.
- **Best strategy**: Be observant, compare each room with its normal appearance, and never trust a visitor without checking their behavior.
- **Critical tip**: Recheck the house after each encounter to detect anomalies early.
- **Top choice**: Stay alert, avoid unnecessary risks, and follow the inspection cycle to reach the ending.

## Introduction to Home Alone Anomaly
Home Alone Anomaly is a Roblox horror game developed by 3 AM Productions & Freeground. Players find themselves in a house where they must check each visitor, identify suspicious anomalies, and make decisions that could mean the difference between survival and failure. The game emphasizes observation, decision-making, and staying calm under pressure.

## Walkthrough
### Learn the Normal House Layout
Before the night begins, inspect the rooms, doors, windows, furniture, and decorations. Remembering the normal layout will help you recognize changes later in the game.

### Begin the Night Routine
Move through the house and complete available interactions while listening for door knocks, movement, and other audio cues. Return to the main areas regularly to stay alert.

### Inspect Every Visitor Carefully
When someone appears at the door, observe their appearance and read all dialogue before making a decision. Look for unnatural behavior, inconsistent statements, or details that do not match the situation.

### Check the House After Each Encounter
After dealing with a visitor, inspect the interior again. Anomalies may appear as changed objects, unusual figures, altered lighting, or unexpected movement.

### Avoid Dangerous Interactions
Do not approach suspicious figures or interact with clearly abnormal events unless the game requires it. Create distance and use the safest available response when an encounter becomes threatening.

### Continue the Observation Cycle
Repeat the door check, dialogue review, and house inspection process as the night progresses. Later encounters demand closer attention because the warning signs can become less obvious.

### Handle the Final Encounters
Near the end of the night, use everything learned from earlier visitors and anomalies. Reject clearly unsafe choices and avoid taking unnecessary risks before the final sequence begins.

### Reach the Final Scene
Survive the remaining sequence without inviting danger inside or approaching active threats. The final scene begins after the required encounters and inspection stages have been completed.

## Visitors Guide
### Normal-Looking Visitor
A visitor may look ordinary and speak calmly without showing an immediate visual anomaly. Their explanation may still be vague, inconsistent, or unrelated to the reason they came to the house. Read every dialogue line and only continue when both their appearance and story remain consistent.

### Familiar-Looking Visitor
Some visitors may appear recognizable or attempt to gain trust through familiarity. Unnatural expressions, strange wording, unusual timing, or behavior that does not match the person they resemble should raise suspicion. Judge the visitor by current evidence rather than recognition and refuse entry when details do not match.

### Visitor With Abnormal Features
A dangerous visitor can display distorted facial details, unnatural proportions, or other visible abnormalities. Eyes, mouth, posture, movement, or body shape appear noticeably wrong. Do not allow the visitor inside and avoid prolonged interaction after identifying the anomaly.

### Aggressive Visitor
An unsafe visitor may pressure the player, become threatening, or demand an immediate response. Hostile language, repeated demands, sudden changes in tone, or attempts to prevent careful inspection should be treated as a red flag. Do not respond to pressure by opening the door; choose the option that keeps the house secured.

### Visitor With an Inconsistent Story
The visitor gives a reason for arriving, but parts of the explanation conflict with their behavior or the situation. Contradictory dialogue, missing details, or statements that change during the conversation should be treated as a danger signal and deny access.

### Silent or Unresponsive Visitor
A person may remain unusually quiet or fail to respond normally during the door interaction. Long silence, fixed staring, limited dialogue, or no believable explanation for being present should raise suspicion. Keep the door closed and end the encounter without approaching further.

### Visitor Appearing at an Unusual Time
A visitor can arrive when their presence does not fit the current point in the night. The timing feels unexplained, the visitor acts as though the situation is normal, or their reason for arriving is weak. Use the arrival time as an additional clue and reject the visitor when it conflicts with their story.

### Visitor Followed by a House Change
The danger may become clear only after the conversation ends and the player returns inside. New sounds, altered rooms, unusual figures, changed lighting, or objects that were not present before should prompt an immediate inspection of the house.

## Anomalies Guide
### Unexpected Figure
A person or human-like shape appears in a place that was previously empty. The figure remains motionless, watches the player, appears suddenly, or has unnatural features. Do not approach it unnecessarily and follow the safest available route away from the area.

### Changed Furniture or Objects
Furniture, decorations, or household items appear moved, replaced, missing, or visually altered. An object no longer matches its original position, shape, number, or appearance. Treat the change as an active anomaly and avoid interacting with it unless progression requires it.

### Abnormal Face or Body
A character displays facial or physical details that do not look human or consistent. Distorted eyes, mouth, limbs, posture, proportions, or expressions should prompt immediate rejection of the visitor or moving away from the character.

### Lighting Change
The brightness, color, or behavior of the lights changes without a normal cause. Sudden darkness, flickering, uneven lighting, or a room looking different from the previous inspection should prompt an inspection from a safe distance and check nearby rooms for additional changes.

### Window Anomaly
A figure, movement, or visual detail appears outside or directly against a window. A face, silhouette, unexplained motion, or object appears where nothing was visible before. Do not move directly toward the window and remain alert for a related encounter inside.

### Door Anomaly
A door behaves differently, reveals something unusual, or becomes connected to a suspicious visitor event. Unexpected opening, repeated knocking, unusual sounds, or a dangerous figure visible nearby should prompt keeping the entrance secured and inspecting before choosing any interaction.

### Unexplained Sound
Footsteps, knocking, movement, or another sound occurs without a visible source. The sound repeats, moves between rooms, or begins immediately after a visitor encounter. Check nearby areas carefully without rushing toward the source.

### Environment No Longer Matches
Multiple details in a room change together, making the space feel noticeably different from its original state. Altered objects, lighting, figures, or room details appear during the same inspection. Leave the area, avoid suspicious interactions, and continue only after identifying a safe route.

## Endings Guide
### How Do You Reach the Main Ending?
Complete the night sequence while keeping the house secure, handling visitor encounters safely, and surviving each anomaly event. Continue following the inspection cycle until the final scripted scene begins.

### What Happens If You Trust the Wrong Visitor?
Allowing a suspicious or abnormal visitor to gain access can trigger a dangerous encounter and end the run before the final scene. Check appearance, dialogue, and behavior before making the decision.

### Can a Familiar-Looking Person Be Dangerous?
Yes. Familiarity is not enough to confirm that a visitor is safe. Unnatural features, inconsistent dialogue, or strange behavior should take priority over the visitor's appearance.

### What Causes an Early Failure?
Common causes include opening the door for an unsafe visitor, approaching a threatening figure, ignoring an obvious environmental change, or selecting a risky interaction during an encounter.

### Why Should You Inspect the House Repeatedly?
New anomalies can appear after visitor conversations and other scripted events. Rechecking the same rooms allows the player to notice changes before entering a dangerous area.

### What Is the Safest Visitor Strategy?
Keep the door closed until the visitor's appearance, dialogue, and reason for arriving all make sense. Refuse entry whenever one of those details appears abnormal or contradictory.

### How Do You Survive the Final Sequence?
Avoid unnecessary exploration, maintain distance from suspicious figures, and continue choosing the safest available responses. Do not assume the danger has ended until the final scene has fully started.

### What Should You Do After a Failed Run?
Repeat the night while focusing on the encounter that caused the failure. Memorize the normal room layout, slow down during visitor dialogue, and inspect the house after every major event.

## Survival Tips
- **Never Decide Immediately**: Wait until you have inspected the visitor and read the full conversation. A rushed door choice can end the run before important warning signs become clear.
- **Check the Visitor's Appearance**: Look closely for unusual facial features, body details, movement, or other visual inconsistencies before allowing anyone into the house.
- **Read Every Dialogue Line**: Visitor dialogue can provide clues about identity, intentions, or contradictions. Skipping text makes suspicious encounters harder to judge.
- **Compare Repeated Encounters**: A familiar visitor should not automatically be trusted. Compare their current appearance and behavior with earlier encounters because small differences may matter.
- **Recheck the House**: Environmental changes can appear between visitor encounters. Inspect nearby rooms and visible objects instead of remaining at the door for the entire night.
- **Use Sound as a Warning**: Pay attention to knocks, movement, and other audio cues. Unexpected sounds can signal a new visitor or a change somewhere inside the house.
- **Remember Previous Clues**: Keep track of what visitors said and how the house looked earlier. Later choices may depend on recognizing a contradiction or repeated anomaly.
- **Stay Near a Clear Route**: Learn the shortest route between the main rooms and the entrance. Moving confidently through the house makes repeated inspections faster and safer.

## Controls and Mechanics
### Movement
Use WASD or the left analog stick to move through the house and approach interactive areas. Movement is used to inspect rooms, return to the entrance, and investigate changes.

### Look Around
Use mouse movement or the right analog stick to examine visitors, objects, and the surrounding environment. Careful visual inspection helps reveal suspicious details and environmental anomalies.

### Interact
Follow the on-screen interaction prompt to use doors, examine available objects, and begin visitor interactions. Interaction prompts appear when the player is close enough to a usable object or encounter point.

### Inspect the Door
Approach the entrance and use the available prompt to observe the person waiting outside before making a decision. The door encounter is the main point where visitor information and visual clues are presented.

### Advance Dialogue
Select or confirm the displayed dialogue option to continue conversations and reveal additional information. Dialogue can contain clues, contradictions, and information needed to judge a visitor.

### Choose a Response
Select one of the displayed choices to decide how to handle the current visitor or event. Choices affect whether a visitor is accepted, rejected, or handled through another available response.

### Check the Environment
Move between rooms and inspect visible details to look for changes that appeared after an encounter. The house can provide additional warning signs beyond the visitor standing at the door.

### Continue the Night
Complete the current encounter and advance to the next visitor or story event. The survival loop repeats through inspection, dialogue, decision-making, and environmental checking.
