# Home Alone Anomaly: Walkthrough, Visitor Guide, Anomaly Clues, Endings, and Survival Tips

## 🚨 Quick Guide
- **Primary keyword**: Home Alone Anomaly
- **Key mechanic**: Visitor inspection, anomaly detection, and night survival
- **Best strategy**: Thoroughly inspect visitors, detect anomalies, and make informed decisions
- **Critical tip**: Keep the door closed and stay alert throughout the game
- **Top choice**: Prioritize safety over curiosity

## 🌟 Introduction to Home Alone Anomaly
Home Alone Anomaly is a Roblox horror game that puts players in the shoes of a lone character facing unexpected visitors and suspicious anomalies throughout the night. The game is centered around visitor inspection, anomaly detection, and making informed decisions to survive the night.

## 🏡 Understanding the House Layout
Before the night begins, it's important to familiarize yourself with the house layout. Inspect the rooms, doors, windows, furniture, and decorations. Remembering the normal layout will make it easier to recognize any changes later in the game.

### 🛡️ Door Inspection Routine
When a visitor appears at the door, it's crucial to inspect them carefully. Observe their appearance, read all dialogue, and look for any signs of suspicious behavior. Always keep the door closed until you are confident that the visitor is safe.

## 🧐 Visitor Guide
### Normal-Looking Visitor
A visitor may look ordinary and speak calmly. However, be cautious and read every dialogue line. Their explanation may still be vague, inconsistent, or unrelated to the reason they came to the house.

### Familiar-Looking Visitor
Some visitors may appear recognizable or attempt to gain trust through familiarity. However, do not rely on recognition alone. Look for unnatural expressions, strange wording, unusual timing, or behavior that does not match the person they resemble.

### Visitor With Abnormal Features
A dangerous visitor can display distorted facial details, unnatural proportions, or other visible abnormalities. Do not allow the visitor inside and avoid prolonged interaction after identifying the anomaly.

### Aggressive Visitor
An unsafe visitor may pressure the player, become threatening, or demand an immediate response. Do not respond to pressure by opening the door; choose the option that keeps the house secured.

### Visitor With an Inconsistent Story
The visitor gives a reason for arriving, but parts of the explanation conflict with their behavior or the situation. Treat contradictions as a danger signal and deny access.

### Silent or Unresponsive Visitor
A person may remain unusually quiet or fail to respond normally during the door interaction. Keep the door closed and end the encounter without approaching further.

### Visitor Appearing at an Unusual Time
A visitor can arrive when their presence does not fit the current point in the night. Use the arrival time as an additional clue and reject the visitor when it conflicts with their story.

### Visitor Followed by a House Change
The danger may become clear only after the conversation ends and the player returns inside. Inspect the house immediately after every encounter and respond to any new anomaly before continuing.

## 🌩️ Anomaly Detection
Anomalies can appear through visual changes, suspicious characters, unusual sounds, or events that break the normal pattern of the house. Compare each room with its normal state and react when people, objects, lighting, or sounds change unexpectedly.

### Unexpected Figure
A person or human-like shape appears in a place that was previously empty. Do not approach it unnecessarily and follow the safest available route away from the area.

### Changed Furniture or Objects
Furniture, decorations, or household items appear moved, replaced, missing, or visually altered. Treat the change as an active anomaly and avoid interacting with it unless progression requires it.

### Abnormal Face or Body
A character displays facial or physical details that do not look human or consistent. Reject the visitor or move away from the character immediately.

### Lighting Change
The brightness, color, or behavior of the lights changes without a normal cause. Inspect the area from a safe distance and check nearby rooms for additional changes.

### Window Anomaly
A figure, movement, or visual detail appears outside or directly against a window. Do not move directly toward the window and remain alert for a related encounter inside.

### Door Anomaly
A door behaves differently, reveals something unusual, or becomes connected to a suspicious visitor event. Keep the entrance secured and inspect before choosing any interaction.

### Unexplained Sound
Footsteps, knocking, movement, or another sound occurs without a visible source. Check nearby areas carefully without rushing toward the source.

### Environment No Longer Matches
Multiple details in a room change together, making the space feel noticeably different from its original state. Leave the area, avoid suspicious interactions, and continue only after identifying a safe route.

## 🎯 Endings Guide
The final outcome depends on surviving the visitor encounters and responding correctly to dangerous anomalies. Reaching the ending requires consistent decisions throughout the night rather than one final choice.

### How Do You Reach the Main Ending?
Complete the night sequence while keeping the house secure, handling visitor encounters safely, and surviving each anomaly event. Continue following the inspection cycle until the final scripted scene begins.

### What Happens If You Trust the Wrong Visitor?
Allowing a suspicious or abnormal visitor to gain access can trigger a dangerous encounter and end the run before the final scene. Check appearance, dialogue, and behavior before making the decision.

### Can a Familiar-Looking Person Be Dangerous?
Yes. Familiarity is not enough to confirm that a visitor is safe. Unnatural features, inconsistent dialogue, or strange behavior should take priority over the visitor's appearance.

### What Causes an Early Failure?
Common causes include opening the door for an unsafe visitor, approaching a threatening figure, ignoring an obvious environmental change, or selecting a risky interaction during an encounter.

### Why Should You Inspect the House Repeatedly?
New anomalies can appear after visitor conversations and other scripted events. Rechecking the same rooms allows the player to notice changes before entering a dangerous area.

### What Is the Safest Visitor Strategy?
Keep the door closed until the visitor's appearance, dialogue, and reason for arriving all make sense. Refuse entry whenever one of those details appears abnormal or contradictory.

### How Do You Survive the Final Sequence?
Avoid unnecessary exploration, maintain distance from suspicious figures, and continue choosing the safest available responses. Do not assume the danger has ended until the final scene has fully started.

### What Should You Do After a Failed Run?
Repeat the night while focusing on the encounter that caused the failure. Memorize the normal room layout, slow down during visitor dialogue, and inspect the house after every major event.

## 🎯 Survival Tips
Survival depends on patience and observation rather than fast reactions. The safest approach is to inspect every visitor, compare new events with earlier scenes, and avoid treating repeated encounters as automatically identical.

### Never Decide Immediately
Wait until you have inspected the visitor and read the full conversation. A rushed door choice can end the run before important warning signs become clear.

### Check the Visitor's Appearance
Look closely for unusual facial features, body details, movement, or other visual inconsistencies before allowing anyone into the house.

### Read Every Dialogue Line
Visitor dialogue can provide clues about identity, intentions, or contradictions. Skipping text makes suspicious encounters harder to judge.

### Compare Repeated Encounters
A familiar visitor should not automatically be trusted. Compare their current appearance and behavior with earlier encounters because small differences may matter.

### Recheck the House
Environmental changes can appear between visitor encounters. Inspect nearby rooms and visible objects instead of remaining at the door for the entire night.

### Use Sound as a Warning
Pay attention to knocks, movement, and other audio cues. Unexpected sounds can signal a new visitor or a change somewhere inside the house.

### Remember Previous Clues
Keep track of what visitors said and how the house looked earlier. Later choices may depend on recognizing a contradiction or repeated anomaly.

### Stay Near a Clear Route
Learn the shortest route between the main rooms and the entrance. Moving confidently through the house makes repeated inspections faster and safer.
